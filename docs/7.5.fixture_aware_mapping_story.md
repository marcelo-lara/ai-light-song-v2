# EPIC 7 - Story 7.5

## Fixture-Aware Orchestration and Lighting Score Generation

## Goal

Convert abstract lighting behaviors into fixture-aware event plans and a fully populated `lighting_score.md` based on the rig definition.

The score must preserve exact deterministic cue times while also encoding how repeated motifs, harmonic patterns, and event-driven fixture regroupings should repeat, vary, or escalate across the show.

## Inputs

- fixture-agnostic lighting events from Story 7.4
- canonical or reviewed event outputs from Epic 5, especially named energy events from Story 5.4 and the compact event timeline from Story 5.8 when available
- unified analysis context from Story 7.3 when needed for explanation text
- fixture definitions from `data/fixtures/fixtures.json`
- the stable score contract in `docs/lighting_score_template.md`

The upstream inputs must already include exact section, phrase, accent, and pattern-occurrence times. Story 7.5 must not approximate those timings in prose.

Epic 5 events do not replace the Story 7.4 cue anchors. They provide additional deterministic triggers for temporary fixture regroupings, focal targets, and callback logic.

## Dependencies

- Rig-role classification logic
- Event-to-role overlay logic
- Fixture capability mapping
- Score population rules

## Processing Steps

1. Load the fixture configuration and classify fixtures into stable roles.
2. Load relevant Epic 5 event outputs and resolve event-driven orchestration overlays tied to exact event IDs, event windows, and cue anchors.
3. Translate abstract scene and behavior instructions into fixture-specific event assignments, allowing supported events to temporarily regroup fixtures into dynamic formations.
4. Respect fixture type, location, base channel constraints, and capability limits when assigning focus targets, motion, color, or intensity behavior.
5. Generate DMX-ready or DMX-near event data, including explicit overlay or target metadata when a moment requires fixtures to converge, mirror, widen, or isolate.
6. Populate `lighting_score.md` using the canonical template structure, preserving exact timing anchors and repeated-pattern, motif, and event-callback rules.

## Fixture Role Contract

The sample rig supports at least these stable derived roles:

| Role | Example fixtures |
| --- | --- |
| moving_head_main | `mini_beam_prism_l`, `mini_beam_prism_r` |
| moving_head_fx | `head_el150` |
| parcan_inner | `parcan_l`, `parcan_r` |
| parcan_outer | `parcan_pl`, `parcan_pr` |

Stable roles are the default orchestration layer. Story 7.5 must also support dynamic moment-scoped role overlays that temporarily regroup fixtures based on Epic 5 event types while preserving the underlying stable classification.

## Dynamic Role Overlay Contract

- Dynamic roles are temporary event-scoped behaviors layered on top of the stable rig roles. They do not permanently reclassify fixtures.
- Overlays must be keyed to exact Epic 5 event IDs or exact Story 7.4 cue anchors, not approximate prose descriptions.
- Overlays must respect fixture capability boundaries. Only moving fixtures may receive focus-target or pan/tilt convergence instructions.
- Overlays may reference explicit focal targets such as `stage_center` when the rig metadata is sufficient to make that target deterministic.
- When an overlay ends, fixtures must return to their stable role behavior or transition into the next scheduled overlay explicitly.

Examples of supported dynamic overlays:

- On `drop` or `drop_explode`, all moving heads may enter a `moving_head_unison_focus` behavior aimed at `stage_center` or another explicit focal point.
- On `build`, moving heads may tighten into rising or inward motion while parcans hold tension-supporting color.
- On `impact_hit`, all capable fixtures may execute a synchronized hit while preserving fixture-specific control limits.
- On `groove_loop`, stable left-right symmetry may relax into alternating mirror callbacks instead of full-rig unison.

## Lighting Score Requirements

The generated score must populate these sections:

- Title using `# <Song Title> - Lighting Score`
- Metadata
- Feature Summary
- Timing Anchors
- Visual Strategy
- Fixture Intentions, including stable roles and event-driven overlay rules
- Section Plan
- Song-Specific Rules, including repeatable event-to-formation callbacks when supported by the rig

## Acceptance Criteria

- Fixture roles are respected.
- Epic 5 events can trigger deterministic temporary role overlays without breaking the stable rig-role contract.
- The output score is readable by both developers and lighting designers.
- Section transitions and accents remain visible after fixture-specific mapping.
- Exact timing anchors remain explicit enough for an LLM or operator to place cues without guessing.
- Repeated motifs and harmonic patterns are translated into coherent callback or variation rules.
- Event-driven regroupings such as all moving heads converging on a shared target during a drop are represented explicitly when supported by the fixture set.
- Chorus-like high-energy passages read as more intense than intro or release passages.

## Validation

- Confirm the fixture-role classification matches `data/fixtures/fixtures.json`.
- Confirm relevant Epic 5 events are consumed as deterministic orchestration inputs when present, without inventing event times.
- Compare the generated score structure with the canonical template contract in `docs/lighting_score_template.md`.
- Confirm the score title uses the required `# <Song Title> - Lighting Score` format.
- Confirm all score timestamps match the structured timing anchors from Stories 6.3 and 6.4.
- Confirm repeated motif and pattern instructions stay faithful to the symbolic and Layer D artifacts.
- Confirm dynamic role overlays reference exact event IDs, event windows, or cue anchors and return fixtures to stable behavior when the overlay ends.
- Verify that no fixture receives controls it cannot support.

## Failure Cases

- Fixture-aware mapping ignores physical layout or role symmetry
- Fixture-aware mapping ignores canonical Epic 5 events or uses them only as vague prose instead of explicit orchestration rules
- Score prose rounds, shifts, or invents cue times that are absent from the deterministic artifacts
- Score generation produces generic text disconnected from the analyzed song
- Dynamic regrouping assigns unsupported aim or movement behavior to non-moving fixtures
- Fixture-specific constraints are applied inconsistently across repeated sections

## Out of Scope

- Real-time lighting control transport
- Human-in-the-loop live desk editing workflow
