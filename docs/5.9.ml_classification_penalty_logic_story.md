## Goal
Implement the training and enforcement logic for the ML event classifier, moving from simple classification to a "physically honest" system that penalizes predictions lacking grounding in audio transients.

## Core Logic: The Penalty Function
1. **Transient Penalty Logic**:
   - During training/inference, if the model predicts a "Hard Hit" or "Section Boundary" at a timestamp where the `bass_att` or `spectral_flux` is below the local moving average, apply a high penalty score to the loss/confidence.
2. **Neural Signature "Sanity Check"**:
   - Use the "FFT Boundary Audit" (Story 4.5) as a secondary validation. The model is "rewarded" in its alignment score if its predicted transitions align within 50ms of a physical sub-band spike.

## Processing Steps
1. **Contextual Windowing**: Feed the model a "Contextual Window" (e.g., 5 seconds) that includes the volume history and attenuated transients as primary input features.
2. **Loss Function Modification**: Integrate the Physical-Constraint Loss into the backpropagation loop.
3. **Reproducibility**: Ensure the **Mersenne Twister (MT19937)** seed is visible in the logs so "Penalized" scenarios can be perfectly reproduced for tuning.

## Acceptance Criteria
- The training pipeline produces a "Heatmap" lane in the debug logs showing where the AI's prediction was "Penalized" by the physical FFT data.
- Model alignment with physical transients (within 50ms) improves by at least 20% compared to a non-penalized baseline.
- Inference engine uses the "Alignment Score" to scale DMX intensity (Low Score = Penalty = Safe/Dim lights).
