# EPIC 3 - Story 3.1

## Section Segmentation

## Goal

Infer structural song sections from energy and repetition evidence, then snap them to the canonical timing grid.

## Inputs

- Harmonic outputs from EPIC 2
- Low-level energy features from Story 2.6
- Canonical beat and bar grid from Story 1.2
- Optional boundary reference from `data/reference/<Song - Artist>/moises/segments.json` for validation and confidence-gated rescue when available

## Dependencies

- Self-similarity analysis
- Novelty detection
- Rule-based section characterization

## Processing Steps

1. Aggregate harmonic and energy features at beat or bar level.
2. Compute a self-similarity matrix across time.
3. Derive a novelty curve and identify candidate boundaries.
4. **Transient & Dropout Alignment:** Identify the nearest high-confidence physical change within a +/- 1 bar window. This includes energy transients (kick hits) AND negative transients (sudden silence in the Drum or Bass stems).
5. Set the boundary to the exact timestamp of the physical onset or dropout. If a Drum stem dropout is detected at 2:09.042, the section MUST start at exactly 2:09.042, overriding the beat-grid anchor to ensure zero-latency synchronization.
6. Optionally attach heuristic `section_character` labels using energy, repetition, motion, and contrast cues.
7. Assign a confidence score to each section and a boundary-confidence score to each transition.
8. If `moises/segments.json` exists, compare inferred boundaries against the reference as a validation pass. When a boundary falls below the documented confidence gate or fails structural integrity badly enough to trigger rescue, the implementation may promote the reference boundary into the canonical section artifact only if:
   - the inferred boundary remains preserved in a producer-scoped inferred artifact or validation payload,
   - the promoted canonical artifact records the reference source path and the failing quality gate in `generated_from`,
   - the promotion is deterministic and explicit in validation output,
   - section-character labels remain inference-driven unless a future Story explicitly allows reference-label promotion.

## Phase 1 Onset-Anchored Alignment Rule

 - **Primary Anchor:** Physical Transients (Onsets and Dropouts). If a structural change aligns with a measurable spike OR a sudden removal of energy in a specific stem (Drums/Bass), use that exact timestamp.
- **Grid Context:** Use the Story 1.2 beat grid to identify which "Bar" or "Beat" the onset belongs to for metadata purposes, but do not shift the `start` time to match the grid if it causes more than 50ms of latency.
- **Zero-Latency Principle:** It is better to change the light exactly on the kick (19.0s) even if the grid thinks the "beat" is 19.1s.

## Open Tuning Target

- Keep the current novelty-driven beat refinement, but review cases where human perception places the new section slightly after the raw novelty onset.
- In the next adjustment pass, evaluate whether some section starts should anchor to the first full post-transition beat when that better matches the sustained character of the new section, especially for sparse inbreak or breakdown entries.
- Treat this as a boundary-placement refinement problem, not a label remapping shortcut.

## Section Character Heuristics

`section_character` labels are optional metadata in phase 1. The primary requirement is still to annotate stable structural change windows at lighting-useful points.

- `ambient_opening`: restrained opener with lower motion and lower energy
- `vocal_spotlight`: voice-led section where the vocal contour dominates more than rhythm or accompaniment
- `vocal_lift`: voice-led section with stronger energy and emotional lift than a quieter spotlight
- `flowing_plateau`: stable mid-energy window without a sharp structural shock
- `groove_plateau`: active plateau with sustained pulse and repetition-driven momentum
- `momentum_lift`: ramping window with clear forward energy growth
- `focal_lift`: strongest payoff window with the highest energy or repetition pressure
- `contrast_bridge`: contrastive or unstable window suited to a transition into the next state
- `breath_space`: lower-density breathing room or arrangement clearance
- `instrumental_bed`: accompaniment-led or texture-led passage with reduced vocal dominance
- `percussion_break`: percussion-dominant pocket with reduced harmonic and vocal presence
- `release_tail`: final taper or release window

## Output Schema

```json
{
  "sections": [
    {
      "section_id": "section-001",
      "start": 0.0,
      "end": 28.0,
      "label": "ambient_opening",
      "section_character": "ambient_opening",
      "confidence": 0.82
    }
  ]
}
```

The `label` field should carry the lighting-oriented `section_character` string when characterization is available. It may be omitted or set to `null` when the implementation can detect a structural change reliably but cannot characterize the section with confidence.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Boundaries align with human perception within roughly one to two bars.
- **First Section Start:** The first section must always start at `0.0`, regardless of the raw novelty detection onset.
- **Onset-Truth:** Boundaries must use the exact timestamp of the detected physical onset (e.g., kick/snare) to ensure zero-latency transitions. Mathematical grid-snapping is a fallback, not a requirement.
- **Micro-Structure Sensitivity:** 1-bar "breaks" or "pockets" characterized by a sharp drop in energy or drum presence must be identified as distinct sections (e.g., `breath_space`).
- High-energy, sparse, transition, and payoff windows are characterized consistently enough for lighting decisions.
- Boundaries do not cut obvious phrases mid-bar unless the evidence is unusually strong.
- Over-segmentation is avoided.

## Validation

- Compare inferred structural change points against validation-only reference section files when they are available.
- **FFT Boundary Audit:** Use `essentia/fft_bands.json` to verify that boundaries align with the leading edge of spectral transients. A delta > 50ms between a boundary and the nearest sub-band spike is a validation warning.
- Reference section files are optional, read-only validation inputs by default. If confidence-gated rescue is implemented, validation must show both the original inferred boundary and the promoted reference-backed boundary rather than hiding the mismatch.
- When reference labels are noisy, validation should score boundary alignment independently of section-character naming.
- Confirm section IDs and time windows are consistent with later energy and symbolic summaries.
- Confirm section windows cover the full analyzed song span without overlap.

## Failure Cases

- Using only energy and missing repetition structure
- Over-labeling many short sections in repetitive songs
- Snapping boundaries away from the real musical transition
- Locking to the earliest detectable novelty spike when the perceived section entry settles on the following full beat
