# EPIC 6 - Story 6.2
## Unified Perceptual Embedding and Musical Signature extraction

## Goal
Replace noisy, error-prone transcription (MIDI/Drums) and chord detection with a **Unified Deep Perceptual Embedding**. This provides a production-grade "Musical Signature" that is stable, genre-agnostic, and serves as the primary input for both LLM abstractions and ML event inference.

## Inputs
- **Audio Stems**: Vocals, Drums, Bass, Other (Story 1.1).
- **Master Timing**: Beat/Bar grid (Story 1.2).

## Processing Steps
1.  **Deep Feature Extraction**:
    - Pass audio stems through a pre-trained backbone (e.g., VGGish, PANNs, or CLMR).
    - Extract 128-dimensional embeddings every 100ms.
2.  **Rhythmic Saliency & Onset Strength**:
    - Instead of drum transcription, generate a "Rhythmic Energy Profile" from the Drums/Bass stems.
    - Measure "Pulse Clarity" to detect sections where the beat is driving vs. ambient.
3.  **Harmonic Tension Proxy**:
    - Instead of naming chords (Cmaj vs. Cmin), calculate the **Vector Drift** between consecutive embeddings.
    - High similarity = Harmonic Stability; High drift = Harmonic Transition.
4.  **Musical Signature Compression**:
    - Use PCA or a lightweight autoencoder to compress high-dimensional data into a 16-channel "Musical Signature" vector for easier processing in downstream Epic 5 layers.

## Output Artifacts
- `data/artifacts/<Song>/layer_perceptual_embedding.json`: The raw 128-d vectors.
- `data/artifacts/<Song>/layer_musical_signature.json`: The compressed 16-channel timeline.

## Acceptance Criteria
- **Temporal Stability**: Feature streams must not "flicker" or oscillate in ways that would cause erratic lighting triggers.
- **Production Reliability**: The extraction must succeed on complex mixes where traditional transcription (Story 3.1/3.2) failed.
- **ML Compatibility**: The output must be perfectly aligned to the Story 1.2 timeline for Story 5.2 normalization.
