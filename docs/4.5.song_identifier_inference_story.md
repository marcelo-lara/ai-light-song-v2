# EPIC 4 - Story 4.5
## [PENDING] Song Identifier Inference and Physical Transient Audit

## Goal

Infer high-value song moments and named energy events from the derived energy layer so downstream consumers can reason about moments such as drops, explosive releases, chilldowns, and other recurring identifiers without depending on hand-written labels.

This story formalizes a controlled identifier vocabulary, starting with the drop concept defined in [drop_definition.md](source references/drop_definition.md). Additional identifier types may be added later, but they must be explicitly documented before they are emitted by the pipeline.

## Intent

This story has two responsibilities only:

1. infer documented identifier windows from energy-led evidence
2. attach event-level physical transient audit metadata that tells downstream consumers how well each inferred event aligns with measurable FFT activity

It does not define downstream lighting policy. Later stories may consume the audit metadata, but they own the consumer behavior.

## Inputs

- Event vocabulary and schema from Story 5.1
- Normalized event feature inputs from Story 4.4 when available
- Derived energy features from Story 4.1
- Canonical section windows from Story 3.1
- Optional symbolic or harmonic context when it is needed for disambiguation, but not as the primary detection signal

## Dependencies

- Event names must remain within the documented canonical vocabulary.
- Stable section windows and energy cards
- Deterministic thresholds or rule sets for named event inference
- An explicit identifier vocabulary with per-identifier definitions and minimum evidence requirements
- A stable transient-audit method that can score event anchors against measurable sub-band activity

## Processing Steps

1. Load `layer_c_energy.json` as the primary continuous analysis input.
2. Infer candidate identifier windows using energy-derived evidence as the primary signal and symbolic or harmonic context only when it is required for disambiguation.
3. Perform a physical transient audit for each accepted identifier:
  - cross-check the identifier anchor against sub-band transients and attenuated features such as `bass_att`
  - identify the nearest leading edge in `fft_bands.json`
  - compute an `alignment_score` based on how closely the identifier anchor matches a physical transient, with a target lock window of 50 ms
4. Attach confidence, evidence summaries, audit metadata, section references, and exact anchor times to each accepted identifier.
5. Flag mismatches where an event implies a strong transition or impact but `bass_att` or `spectral_flux` remains below the relevant local moving average.
6. Write the results to `energy_summary/hints.json`.
7. Expose only identifiers whose definitions are documented and validated; unknown or experimental labels must not be emitted silently.

## Initial Vocabulary Rule

The first required identifier in this story is:

- `drop`: a high-confidence transition into a high-energy section characterized by coordinated increases in loudness, rhythmic activation, spectral expansion, and bass activation, as defined in [drop_definition.md](source references/drop_definition.md).

Identifiers that may be added later, but are not automatically in scope until separately defined, include:

- `drop_explode`
- `chilldown`
- `build`
- `fake_drop`
- `anthem_call`
- `impact_hit`
- `release_reset`

## Acceptance Criteria

- The story owns named energy-event inference such as `drop` and other explicitly documented song identifiers.
- The implementation uses energy-derived evidence as the primary basis for these identifiers.
- Every emitted identifier has a documented definition, confidence value, evidence summary, and event-level audit metadata.
- Every emitted identifier is valid against the canonical event vocabulary contract.
- The output is deterministic for repeated runs on the same inputs.
- The story does not emit undocumented identifier labels.
- The story does not prescribe downstream lighting behavior; it only exposes evidence and scores for later consumers.

## Validation

- Confirm inferred `drop` events satisfy the documented multi-feature evidence requirements from [drop_definition.md](source references/drop_definition.md).
- Confirm event times align with strong transitions in `layer_c_energy.json` and section boundaries when applicable.
- Confirm unsupported identifiers are absent from `supported_identifiers` and from `events[]`.
- Confirm downstream consumers can trace each identifier back to section and evidence fields without guessing.
- Confirm the audit metadata reports how closely the identifier anchor aligns with a physical transient.
- Verify that an event implying a strong impact or release is flagged when supporting bass transients are absent.

## Failure Cases

- A `drop` label is emitted from a single weak feature spike with no coordinated release evidence.
- The implementation emits undocumented identifiers because they were convenient at runtime.
- Event timing is approximate prose rather than traceable timestamps.
- The story collapses all high-energy moments into `drop` without distinguishing their evidence patterns.
- The story mixes producer-side audit generation with downstream lighting policy, making the ownership ambiguous.

## Out of Scope

- Fixture-specific lighting decisions
- Human editing of the identifier artifact itself
- Freeform genre storytelling without a documented identifier contract
