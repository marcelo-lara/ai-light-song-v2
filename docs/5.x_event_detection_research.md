# Epic 5: Event Detection Research & Development

## Objective
Review, validate, and improve the event detection engine (Epic 5). The baseline uses static thresholds (`event_rules.py`), which historically misfires on songs with non-standard mixing or continuous energy shifts (e.g., "ayuni"). By utilizing a reference-annotated song ("What a Feeling - Courtney Storm") and the problematic outlier ("ayuni"), this I+D (Research & Development) iteration explores more generic, robust, and adaptive algorithms for labeling structural and energetic events (drops, buildups, breakdowns).

## Baseline Benchmark
**Method**: Static empirical thresholds applied to simple moving energy deltas and frequency band variances (`event_rules.py`).
**Limitations**: 
- Relies on "magic numbers" (e.g., `build_energy_delta >= 0.08`).
- Breaks on over-compressed tracks or highly dynamic tracks.
- High false positive rate for "build" and "drop" on tracks with prominent melodic changes but minimal low-end shifts.

## Alternative Approaches to Evaluate

### Alternative 1: Dynamic Rolling Thresholds (Adaptive Baseline)
Rather than a global absolute threshold (e.g., `0.08`), calculate the rolling mean and variance of the signal energy over a large window (e.g., past 16 or 32 bars). 
- An event candidate is triggered when the local energy or novelty delta exceeds `N * standard_deviations` from the rolling mean.
- **Advantage**: Adjusts to the track's master loudness and local dynamic range automatically.

### Alternative 2: Spectral Novelty / Peak Picking
Using local novelty functions derived from spectral flux or MFCCs, aggregated at block or section boundaries. 
- **Advantage**: A proven signal-processing methodology (via `librosa` or `essentia`) explicitly designed to locate points of maximum structural change.

### Alternative 3: K-Means State Clustering
Analyze the feature space (bass, mid, high, loudness, tempo) and cluster each 4-bar or 8-bar chunk into $K$ states (e.g., $K=3$ for Low, Medium, High energy sections).
- Transitions between these discrete states form the basis of the rule candidates.
- **Advantage**: Looks at the song holistically (unsupervised learning) so the definition of a "Drop" is contextually scaled to whatever the highest energy cluster is for *that specific song*.

## Validation Strategy
We rely on:
1. `benchmark_event_outputs` scoring against reference annotations for "What a Feeling".
2. Subjective / JSON footprint review for "ayuni".

### Experiment 1 Results (Alternative 1: Dynamic Rolling Thresholds / Z-Scores)
**Status**: Implemented and benchmarked.
**Method**: Replaced static threshold magic numbers (`0.08`, etc.) with dynamic standard deviation ranges (Z-scores) calculated from the whole track's signal properties (`energy_delta`, `tension_mean`, etc.).
**Findings**:
- "What a Feeling": 133 candidate events. 3/3 matched initial ground truth landmarks (layer_add, build, drop).
- "ayuni": 72 candidate events.
- **Conclusion**: The adaptive logic successfully triggers on necessary dynamic landmarks without crashing or missing them. However, it generates a very high number of candidate events (high sensitivity / high potential false positive rate prior to manual annotations).

### Experiment 2 Results (Alternative 2: Spectral Novelty / Peak Picking Hybrid)
**Status**: Implemented and benchmarked alongside Alternative 1.
**Method**: Added local sequence maxima/minima isolation (`_is_peak` radius tests) instead of raw boundary comparisons for impulsive events (`impact_hit`, `layer_add`, `layer_remove`).
**Findings**:
- "What a Feeling": Machine events reduced from 133 to 108 (approx ~20% decrease in false-positives), while preserving 3/3 ground-truth landmarks.
- "ayuni": Reduced from 72 machine events to 57 machine events.
- **Conclusion**: By enforcing a standard radius minimum/maximum (e.g. 4 beats, approx 1 bar) before validating a density or energy spike, we stop the engine from creating contiguous "clusters" of layer_add/layer_remove events across slightly sloping sections. This allows downstream timelines (`song_event_timeline.json`) to remain adequately compact for LLM context windows (as required by Epic Story 5.8).

### Experiment 3 Results (Alternative 3: K-Means State Clustering Filtering)
**Status**: Implemented on top of Alternative 1 & 2.
**Method**: Used `scikit-learn` to apply $K$-Means Clustering ($K=3$) using a 3D feature matrix per beat (`[energy_score, bass_activation, onset_density]`). The clusters were then mathematically sorted into "Low=0", "Mid=1", and "High=2" states based on their centroids. We then applied constraint logic inside `event_rules.py` (e.g. `drop` candidates MUST only generate if the beat belongs to cluster 2 "High", and `pause` events MUST belong to cluster 0 "Low").
**Findings**:
- "What a Feeling": Maintained 3/3 Ground Truths. False-positives dropped to 103 (-5).
- "ayuni": Machine events successfully dropped from 57 down to 52 (-5 over the last run, and -20 from baseline)!
- **Conclusion**: K-Means clustering does an amazing job at contextually normalizing a uniquely mixed track. It successfully threw away "fake drops" on `ayuni` because those moments mathematically grouped into the "Mid" energy cluster for that specific song, rather than crossing a static magic limit. 

This hybrid engine (Alt 1 + Alt 2 + Alt 3) produces the most accurate and dense configuration yet!

*End of R&D experimentation for Event Rules generation.*
