# EPIC 5 - Story 5.4

## Song Identifier Inference

## Goal

Infer high-value song moments and named energy events from the derived energy layer so downstream lighting logic can reason about moments such as drops, explosive releases, chilldowns, and other recurring show-design identifiers without depending on hand-written labels.

This story formalizes a controlled identifier vocabulary, starting with the drop concept defined in [drop_definition.md](docs/source references/drop_definition.md). Additional identifier types may be added later, but they must be explicitly documented before they are emitted by the pipeline.

## Inputs

- Event vocabulary and schema from Story 5.1
- Normalized event feature inputs from Story 5.2 when available
- Derived energy features from Story 3.3
- Canonical section windows from Story 3.2
- Optional symbolic or harmonic context when it is needed for disambiguation, but not as the primary detection signal

## Dependencies

- Event names must remain within the documented canonical vocabulary.
- Stable section windows and energy cards
- Deterministic thresholds or rule sets for named event inference
- An explicit identifier vocabulary with per-identifier definitions and minimum evidence requirements

## Processing Steps

1. Load `layer_c_energy.json` as the primary continuous analysis input.
2. Evaluate contiguous beat windows for a combined release of loudness, rhythmic activation, and general energy matching the definition of a `drop`, using an 8-second debounce window to prevent clustering.
3. Cross-reference the identified anchor time against the canonical section windows purely as a lightweight metadata lookup to populate the schema.
4. Attach confidence, evidence summaries, section references, and exact anchor times to each accepted identifier.
5. Write the producer-scoped result to `data/artifacts/<Song - Artist>/energy_summary/hints.json`.
6. Preserve the distinction between raw energy evidence and higher-level named identifiers so downstream consumers can explain why an event was inferred.
7. Expose only identifiers whose definitions are documented and validated; unknown or experimental labels must not be emitted silently.

## Initial Vocabulary Rule

The first required identifier in this story is:

- `drop`: a high-confidence transition into a high-energy section characterized by coordinated increases in loudness, rhythmic activation, spectral expansion, and bass activation, as defined in [drop_definition.md](/home/darkangel/ai-light-song-v2/docs/drop_definition.md).

Identifiers that may be added later, but are not automatically in scope until separately defined, include:

- `drop_explode`
- `chilldown`
- `build`
- `fake_drop`
- `anthem_call`
- `impact_hit`
- `release_reset`

## Acceptance Criteria

- The story owns named energy-event inference such as `drop` and other explicitly documented song identifiers.
- The implementation uses energy-derived evidence as the primary basis for these identifiers.
- Every emitted identifier has a documented definition, confidence value, and evidence summary.
- Every emitted identifier is valid against the canonical event vocabulary contract.
- The output is deterministic for repeated runs on the same inputs.
- The story does not emit undocumented identifier labels.

## Validation

- Confirm inferred `drop` events satisfy the documented multi-feature evidence requirements from [drop_definition.md](/home/darkangel/ai-light-song-v2/docs/drop_definition.md).
- Confirm event times align with strong transitions in `layer_c_energy.json` and section boundaries when applicable.
- Confirm unsupported identifiers are absent from `supported_identifiers` and from `events[]`.
- Confirm downstream consumers can trace each identifier back to section and evidence fields without guessing.

## Failure Cases

- A `drop` label is emitted from a single weak feature spike with no coordinated release evidence.
- The implementation emits undocumented identifiers because they were convenient at runtime.
- Event timing is approximate prose rather than traceable timestamps.
- The story collapses all high-energy moments into `drop` without distinguishing their evidence patterns.

## Out of Scope

- Fixture-specific lighting decisions
- Human editing of the identifier artifact itself
- Freeform genre storytelling without a documented identifier contract
