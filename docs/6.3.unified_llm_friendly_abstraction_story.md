# EPIC 6 - Story 6.3
## Unified LLM-Friendly Abstraction

## Goal

Produce one deterministic, schema-driven `song_map.json` artifact that consolidates validated structure, symbolic summaries, energy context, and identifier hints into a prompt-friendly representation.

The artifact must be readable by a human and directly consumable by an LLM without requiring additional clarification about field meaning, score semantics, or ownership.

## Intent

This story is the prompt-facing handoff contract.

It does not perform a second analysis pass. It summarizes already-validated upstream artifacts into controlled vocabulary, explicit numbers, and stable descriptions.

## Inputs

- section windows and confidence metadata from Story 3.1 and Story 3.2
- aligned phrase information from Story 3.4
- symbolic summaries from Story 4.3
- derived energy summaries from Story 4.1
- identifier hints and event-level audit metadata from Story 4.5
- optional ML event predictions from Story 5.3 or Story 5.4 when they are already available and validated

## Dependencies

- deterministic descriptor templates and controlled vocabularies
- explicit score semantics for all confidence or alignment fields
- canonical ids for sections, phrases, motifs, and identifiers
- no dependence on an external LLM for baseline generation

## Processing Steps

1. Load the canonical upstream artifacts and reject missing required inputs explicitly.
2. Convert symbolic, energy, and structural metrics into a controlled vocabulary with stable labels.
3. Build section-level summaries that combine:
   - exact timing and section ids
   - energy and confidence summaries
   - concise symbolic descriptions derived from the source metrics
   - notable identifier hints already inferred upstream
4. Build phrase- and motif-level summaries only when the upstream artifacts expose canonical ids and timings.
5. Preserve all numeric evidence needed by downstream prompt consumers, including `confidence_score`, `alignment_score`, and relevant normalized feature values.
6. Emit concise human-readable descriptions that reflect the structured metrics rather than replacing them.
7. Persist the result as a stable JSON artifact optimized for prompt construction and downstream orchestration.

## Output Artifacts

- `data/artifacts/<Song - Artist>/llm_abstraction/song_map.json`

## Acceptance Criteria

- The artifact has one coherent schema and one documented purpose.
- Repeated runs on the same upstream artifacts yield identical descriptions unless the inputs change.
- Every description is traceable back to explicit upstream metrics or identifiers.
- Score meanings are documented in the artifact contract so prompt consumers do not need follow-up clarification.
- Missing optional inputs fail explicitly or remain absent from the output without being silently fabricated.

## Validation

- Compare the text fields against the structured symbolic and energy summaries.
- Confirm section and phrase descriptions reference only evidence present in the source artifacts.
- Confirm `alignment_score` and `confidence_score` meanings are documented and not overloaded.
- Confirm no second independent analysis path is introduced by this story.

## Failure Cases

- Overly generic text that fits every song
- Descriptions contradict the structured symbolic or energy data
- The artifact omits numeric evidence and forces downstream prompt consumers to guess
- The story re-computes identifiers or structure instead of summarizing canonical upstream outputs

## Out of Scope

- Creative lyric interpretation
- Freeform narrative generation
- Replacing the upstream analysis stories
