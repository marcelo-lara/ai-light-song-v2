# EPIC 4 - Story 4.4
## Context-Aware Feature Normalization and ML-Ready Alignment

### Goal
Transform heterogeneous data from upstream layers (audio, symbolic, harmonic, and structural) into a standardized, **ML-ready "Contextual Feature Vector."** This story ensures that "high energy" is defined relative to the specific song's profile, enabling consistent event detection across diverse genres.

### Inputs
- **Audio Features**: Loudness, FFT Bands, Spectral Flux, and Spectral Centroid trends (Stories 1.3, 1.4).
- **Symbolic/Pattern Features**: Rhythmic density, onset strength, and kick/snare patterns (Stories 3.2, 3.3).
- **Harmonic Features**: HPCP, chord stability, and tonal tension proxies (Stories 2.1, 2.4).
- **Structural Context**: Pre-defined sections (Intro, Verse, etc.) and global song statistics (BPM, Mean Energy) (Stories 4.2, 5.4).

### Processing Steps
1.  **Adaptive Normalization (Z-Score & Min-Max)**:
    - Instead of global 0–1 scaling, calculate the **Z-score** for each feature based on the song's global mean and standard deviation. This highlights "anomalies" (e.g., a sudden volume spike) as being $x$ standard deviations from the song's average.
2.  **Time-Smoothed Attenuated Features**:
    - Calculate and store attenuated versions of frequency bands (`bass_att`, `mid_att`).
    - These respond faster to transients and decay smoothly, providing a better feature set for the ML model than raw energy.
3.  **Volume History Normalization**:
    - Use the 2–5 second rolling history buffer from Story 1.4 to normalize loudness features.
2.  **Temporal Delta Calculation**:
    - Compute the first-order derivative ($\Delta$) for energy and density. This captures the **rate of change**, which is more predictive of a "Build-up" or "Drop" than the raw value itself.
3.  **Multi-Scale Rolling Windows**:
    - Generate three parallel feature streams:
        - **Local (Instantaneous)**: 1-beat window for transients.
        - **Phrasal (Medium)**: 4-bar window for rhythmic stability.
        - **Structural (Long)**: 32-bar window to capture section-wide energy trends.
4.  **Feature Fusion (The Contextual Vector)**:
    - Align all features to a unified 100ms grid.
    - Concatenate local features (including attenuated transients) with their phrasal and structural counterparts to provide "peripheral vision."
5.  **Provenance Metadata**:
    - Tag each vector with the normalization method and source layer IDs to ensure reproducibility.

### Output Artifacts
* `data/artifacts/<Song>/event_inference/contextual_features.json`: A dense time-series of normalized vectors.
* `data/artifacts/<Song>/event_inference/song_statistics.json`: The global mean/variance used for the Z-score normalization.

### Acceptance Criteria
* **Genre Invariance**: An "Energetic" section in a ballad must have a similar Z-score signature to an "Energetic" section in a rock song.
* **Alignment Integrity**: All features (drums, chords, energy) must be synchronized within 10ms of the master audio timeline.
* **ML-Readiness**: Data must be structured as a flat numerical array/matrix suitable for direct ingestion by the Story 5.9 ML Classifier.

### Validation
* Compare normalized output of a high-loudness track (EDM) and a low-loudness track (Classical) to ensure feature ranges are statistically comparable.
* Verify that "silence" features correctly account for ambient noise floors calculated during the song-level statistics phase.
