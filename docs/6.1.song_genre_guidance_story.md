# EPIC 6 - Story 6.1

## Song Genre Guidance

## Goal

Infer one or more coarse song genre labels that can guide downstream reasoning about structure, likely transitions, and what to look for when interpreting parts, while remaining explicit about uncertainty and avoiding invented genre logic.

This story is intended to provide guidance, not to hardcode genre stereotypes into later stages. If the selected genre model is unavailable, fails, or produces an ambiguous output, the genre list must remain `["unknown"]`.

## Inputs

- Source song audio
- Optional stem metadata from Story 1.1 when it helps with provenance
- Optional harmonic, symbolic, or energy summaries for later explanatory notes only, not as a hand-built fallback classifier

## Dependencies

- A documented third-party genre classification model or embedding model with a trained genre head
- The selected model's documented native label vocabulary
- Confidence thresholds and rejection rules

## Model Policy

Use an approved external model, preferably a 2D-CNN architecture optimized for mel-spectrogram features. Do not implement a custom fallback algorithm from handcrafted heuristics if the model fails.

Recommended models to evaluate, in roughly this order:

1. Essentia TensorFlow music-style or genre classifiers when the available label set matches the project needs.
2. `musicnn` tagger checkpoints when used with an explicit genre subset and thresholding policy.
3. OpenL3 or similar pretrained music embeddings only when paired with a separately trained and versioned genre head.

Rules:

- Prefer one primary model and optionally one documented secondary model.
- If no approved model runs successfully, emit `genres: ["unknown"]`.
- If the model's score distribution is too ambiguous to support a clear top label, emit `genres: ["unknown"]`.
- Do not substitute a hand-built rule engine based on BPM, key, chord density, or section labels.
- Do not silently remap model outputs into a project-specific replacement vocabulary.

## Processing Steps

1. Load the full-song audio as the primary classification input.
2. Run the configured approved genre model.
3. Keep the selected model's output labels unchanged.
4. Reject predictions when the score distribution does not support a clear top label under the documented ambiguity rule.
5. Write the result to a producer-scoped artifact and include explanatory notes about confidence, co-winner selection, and model provenance.
6. Expose downstream guidance describing how the inferred genre may influence what analysts should look for when reviewing parts.

## Guidance Intent

When one or more genres are available, they may guide downstream interpretation such as:

- whether drops, breakdowns, chorus lifts, or groove-lock sections are likely to matter
- whether the song is more likely to emphasize harmonic callbacks, rhythmic drive, vocal hooks, or texture shifts
- what kinds of repeated-part structures are worth validating manually

This guidance must remain advisory. Later stages must not assume the genre is correct when the confidence is weak.

## Output Artifacts

- `data/artifacts/<Song - Artist>/genre.json`


## Acceptance Criteria

- The story uses an approved third-party model rather than a custom fallback algorithm.
- Failed inference or ambiguous inference yields `genres: ["unknown"]`.
- The artifact records model provenance, confidence, and candidate predictions.
- The output remains coarse and guidance-oriented rather than pretending to provide fine-grained musicology.

## Validation

- Confirm the configured model actually runs in the supported environment.
- Confirm ambiguous predictions resolve to `unknown`.
- Confirm the emitted selected genre labels belong to the documented allowed vocabulary.
- Confirm downstream guidance text does not overclaim beyond the confidence and label granularity.

## Failure Cases

- A handcrafted heuristic classifier is introduced as a hidden fallback.
- Ambiguous outputs are forced into a concrete genre instead of `unknown`.
- The story remaps model-native labels into a different project-specific vocabulary.
- Downstream stages treat the genre as ground truth instead of advisory context.

## Out of Scope

- Fine-grained subgenre musicology
- Genre-conditioned generation rules without additional evidence
- Custom fallback classifiers built from internal heuristics
